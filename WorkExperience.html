Field Trips


1) The first field trip that I went to was in Sophomore year, to the SAP center. I learnt a lot about what SAP does. They do a variety of things such as create platforms for projects, develop applications, and are into analytics. The SAP center is located in San Ramon and there are lots of opportunities that the company creates for other companies as well as their own. One of their widely known competitors include Oracle.

2) Another field trip that I went to was in Junior year, which was to the SAP center again. This time, instead of taking a tour as a group, I, along with another student, shadowed meetings with an SAP employee, who was going around in 30 minute sessions to talk with other businesses to see how SAP tools could be implemented into the system of certain aspects of each business.

Guest Speakers

1) Maynard Holliday was one influential guest speaker. He talked about Chernobyl, one of the Soviet Union's most advanced nuclear facilities and how it blew up. A lot of radioactive material was spread over a radius of several miles. To help in the cleaning process, Maynard had to map the problem, and create solutions to clean the area up, since the radiation levels were too high for a human to enter the area. Using that information, they had to create the most simplistic robot to enter the area and ready the area for mass cleanup done by humans.
